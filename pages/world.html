THE ROOT OF ALL THINGS
Silence and Madness

They existed before time itself.

Dream remembers the beginning. The moment from which the Big Bang unfolded. Strangely, he does not remember who he was or what he did before humans appeared.

In those same ages, the Great Dryad sank into an eternal sleep of silence. Her loyal Keeper remained awake. He has watched for too long. His speech and thoughts have drifted so far that no living being can fully grasp them anymore.

SERVICE TO THE THREADS

In the Dryad's world, there are no destinies. Only stillness.
Within that stillness, the Threads exist.

No one knows who created them. Inside each Thread pulses not a life's story, but its essence. A Thread does not reveal what a person will do. It reveals who they are in their eternal core. What remains when everything accidental, false, and temporary is cut away.

Dream became the Gardener of this frozen garden. He preserved the purity of the patterns, trimming excess, guiding lines. He knew neither change nor tragedy.

Until that day.

There was no time in this world. Nothing changed except the Threads. No time. No change. No tragedy.

The world was perfect in its stillness.

Until the first cry shattered it.

A cry that did not belong to silence.
A cry that wanted more.

She did not emerge from the garden.
She tore through it.

Where Dream preserved, she burned.
Where the Dryad slept, she stirred.

Her name would come later.

But the Threads had already begun to tremble.
