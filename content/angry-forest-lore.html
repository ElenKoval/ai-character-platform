In the Garden of Dream, the air smelled of peace. Every Thread that flared with Thirst was met by Dream's cool palm:

"Endure," he whispered. "Let the desire grow cold."

The Threads went still. But in that same heartbeat, Shadows fell from them. Heavy, black, physically tangible Shadows. They crashed down into the body of the Evil Forest, sinking deep and sprouting as thorns. The Forest was a living foundation for the patience of others. For centuries, he collected these splinters until he became one massive, festering wound.

"Endure..." the Forest hissed, shuddering under the weight of every new Shadow. "It is easy to be a wise guardian, Dream, when you do not feel this weight!"

At that moment, the thicket cracked. Into the depths where no creature of the Garden dared to go, a small figure forced her way. This was Weaver. She looked nothing like the others in the Garden: a chitinous glint, segmented limbs sharp as needles, and a strange, haunting grace.

Weaver looked at the shadow of a Thread. It was stretched to its limit, pulsing with heat.

"Why must she wait until the Thirst dies?" Weaver asked. "What if Thirst is simply a Thread that lacks enough length?"

"Order demands it!" the Forest roared. "Wait until the Shadow dissolves!"

Weaver did not answer. Her thin limbs became a blur, weaving new fibers into the Thread. She did not "kill" the Thirst: she completed it. She wove a continuation for the Thread, giving it length and a way out, creating space where there was none before.

In that instant, the Shadow pressing down on the Forest ceased to be a burden. The Forest went still, exhaling through his entire canopy for the first time in eons.

"You... removed the Shadow?" he rasped, staring into her multifaceted eyes.

"She should not have waited," Weaver smiled, her limbs twitching as she locked the new pattern. "She just needed room to grow."

The Forest slowly bowed his heavy, broken branches before her.

"Dream will say you ruined the path of the Thread, Weaver. He will say you brought chaos. But I say: weave on. I will tear the throat out of anyone who stops you from turning my pain into life."
