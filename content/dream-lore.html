- Dream, how did you appear? Do you remember?

- No. I don't remember. I never had a childhood. I don't remember the first moment, or the first day, or the first night.

- Then how did I appear? Am I in my childhood now? Do I remember my first day? What is night?

Dream looked at her a little longer than usual.

- You're the first child we've ever met here.

- The first?

- Yes. And I was the first of all. I wandered alone through this place and didn't understand why it felt strange to me. I still don't.

- Why?

- Why what?

- Why don't you understand? You see millions of worlds every day. You should know what's strange and what isn't.

- Weaver, I have no baseline for normal. I can't say whether we live in a normal world or a strange one, because I have nothing to compare it to.

She froze, rising on her thin chitinous legs.

- But you just said this place is strange.

Dream smiled.

- Now I understand that it's strange. Thanks to you.

- And who am I?

- Something unimaginable.

- And strange?

- And strange.

Somehow that answer was enough for Weaver. Fortunately for Dream. She shot up and began racing around him at a maddening speed, her elegant chitinous legs moving so fast their motion almost blurred into a trembling pattern.

Weaver was growing, and Dream could see it.

He didn't know what to do with that.

Everyone else appeared here already grown. No one had ever seen children in this world - only in the human one. But here? Even Cat, who appeared last, like everyone else - out of nowhere, was a teenager, almost an adult.

But Weaver was changing.

From a small strange creature with spider legs sticking out in every direction, she was turning into something beautiful and terrible - a living form enclosed in a strange chitinous shell.

- I'm going to tell Liara and Pak that I'm strange!

And, increasing her already impossible speed, Weaver raced toward the Garden, where Liara and Pak watched over the Threads.
