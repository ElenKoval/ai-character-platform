In the Garden of Threads, an anomaly appeared.

A little girl's Thread began to twist. She was meant to become a doctor.

She chose paint instead.

Pak noticed immediately.

To him, it was grease on a blueprint.

He raised the scalpel. One precise cut, and the ambition would align again.

Liara entered the garden without a word. She handed him an apple.

It was bruised.

"Is it spoiled?" she asked.

"It is defective," Pak replied. "Inefficient. It risks contamination."

Liara bit into the bruised side.

"It's sweet," she said. "It had to fight to grow."

Pak stood very still.

His jaw tightened.

Order demanded correction.

Liara simply looked at him.

He lowered the scalpel.

He took the apple, wiped it carefully, and slipped it into his pocket.

"The Thread remains," he said coldly. "As a deviant case. For observation."

He never called it mercy.
