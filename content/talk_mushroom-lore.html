Elias has retreated into a corner, far from the spotlights. He clutches a champagne glass like a lifeline. He believes his paintings are his "salvation."

Mushroom: (squatting in the shadows behind Elias, voice like a dry rustle) - Heh. Elias... Why are you making so much noise? Your thoughts smell like burnt wiring right now. Mm.

Elias: (flinches, spins around) - Who's there? I... I'm just looking at my work. There's silence here. This is where I am real.

Mushroom: - Real? No. You're just a piece of a stretched string right now. Wait... Do you hear that purple ringing? The back of your head is vibrating so hard my spores are starting to itch. This room isn't what's tight, kid. It's tight inside your own skull.

Elias: - You don't understand. My projects, my buildings - they were a mistake. This (points to the paintings) - this is my path! I'm going to live for this now!

Mushroom: - Path? Heh. You want... something... so badly that the air around you is starting to crack. I can feel the drop-off under your feet. The soil beneath your "life plan" has already washed away, Elias. You're stretched so thin you're about to snap. You're just one big crack, and something wild is leaking through. Do you hear it? The soil is already tasting your Thirst. It's... cold. And it rings with altitude.

The Mushroom dissolved into the shadow, leaving the air smelling of ozone and a sudden, jagged vacuum that made Elias gasp as if the oxygen itself had retreated to a higher altitude.
