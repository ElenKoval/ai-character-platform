A gallery opening in San Francisco. Elias is the "golden boy" of architecture, his buildings winning every prestigious award. Choking on the shallow praise, he slips out to the gallery's back courtyard, where his paintings are displayed in a dark, neglected corner. They are... terrible. Technically perfect, but lifeless. There is no soul in them, only the desperate fear of mediocrity. Elias stands beside them, pale, clutching a glass of champagne. Dream whispers in his ear: "Return to your blueprints. There is peace there. The paintings were a mistake; forget them."

The Intervention

- "They are empty, Elias. Because you aren't painting what you want - you're painting what you want to seem like." A calm, feminine voice cuts through the silence.

Elias flinches.

- "Who are you?" He stares in shock at an impossibly beautiful blonde with vast, unnervingly still eyes.

She barely brushes the canvas with her fingertip, and Elias feels a vibration of pure, unfiltered Desire radiating from her.

- "I am the one who sees that you can overcome mediocrity. Just... follow yourself."

Shiny leaves, leaving Elias staring at his own hands. In that moment, his thread of "Great Architect" snaps with a violent crack. He realizes where his true hunger lies. Within a week, he sells everything: the studio, the collection, the name. He vanishes from the chronicles.

One Year Later

Shiny and her Brother sit in a high-end bar. A news headline flashes on the screen: "Madness on K2: Solo Ascent Without Oxygen in the Dead of Winter." In a grainy photo sits Elias. Frostbitten, exhausted, possessing nothing but that peak. He is sitting on a summit where the air is too thin for life.

Shiny is livid. She slams her phone onto the marble table.

- "I gave him a chance to shine! I thought he'd be the next Da Vinci, owning the minds of millions! And he... he just climbed a piece of ice where no one can see him. He turned his life to ash for a single minute in the cold. He failed."

The Brother slowly sets down his glass of neat bourbon, watching the screen where Elias appears as a tiny but unbearably bright speck.

- "No, sister. You gave him Desire, and he stopped lying. The paintings were just another cage, like the offices. His hunger was for this - a physical triumph over death."

He looks at his sister, his gaze steady as a mountain.

- "Look at him. Up there, he owns the entire world. He didn't trade wealth for scribbles - he traded noise for Greatness. Now he shines brighter than all your diamonds, Shiny. It's just that this light... it isn't for sale."
