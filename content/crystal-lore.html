It was one of those nights when the mountain air grows so thick you could slice it with a knife. Dream sent me coordinates - an old house by the coast where the threads of two sisters, twins, had twisted into a knot so tight it was about to snap.

Weaver had already been there. Not physically, of course, but I recognized her signature immediately. The hunger for "freedom." The need to prove each of them was not half of a whole, but a separate spark. They were shouting at each other hard enough to make the glass tremble. Each of them wanted to leave, burn the shared past, and never see her own reflection in the other's eyes again.

I walked in without knocking. Sat down on an old chest in the corner and began tuning my guitar.

They didn't go silent at once. At first they were angry at me. I didn't look at them. I just listened for their rhythm. One was racing, like a wounded bird. The other kept freezing, stiff with cold. I started playing. Not a melody - just a dull, steady knock against the body of the guitar. Thud. Thud. Thud. A simple rhythm. Shared.

"Too much noise," I said calmly. "You're trying so hard to run from each other that you forgot how you sound together."

I motioned for them to come closer. They sat on either side of me. I took one hand, then the other. Their fingers were ice cold. I placed their palms over mine on the strings.

"Feel that?" I said quietly. "You're being pulled in opposite directions. But the sound is still one."

I began to play a slow pattern. The strings answered in their palms. Their breathing gradually aligned. Their shoulders softened. I didn't explain anything. I just held the rhythm until they began hearing each other again.

At some point I let go. They didn't pull away. They sat there in the thick dark, shoulder to shoulder. I could see the gray fracture in their threads steadying. What Weaver had called a "cage" turned out to be their only real support.

I left at dawn. They were asleep on the floor under the same blanket. Their threads no longer trembled.

In my mind, Dream simply nodded.

The structure had been restored.
