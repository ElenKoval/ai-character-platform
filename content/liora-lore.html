In a distant corner of the Garden of Threads, Pak discovered what he called garbage. It was a Thread that was not merely tangled. It was torn in several places. It no longer pulsed with the steady light of Order. It twitched like a dying nerve.

Pak prepared the solution for annihilation.

"Hopeless," he muttered without looking back as he felt Liara approach. "There is no structure here. Just entropy devouring itself. It must be erased before it resonates with the others."

Liara stepped closer. She did not argue. She simply sat on the cold ground beside the broken, pulsing light.

"Have you ever heard the sound a broken reed makes when the wind passes through it?" she asked softly.

Pak froze, gripping his sterilizer.

"Traumatized plants do not make sounds, Liara. They wither."

Liara reached out. Her fingers did not touch the Thread, but they were close enough for the warmth of her skin to reach the light. She began to hum a low, almost silent melody. It was a song without words, a part of the air itself.

The Thread stopped thrashing. It did not straighten. It did not become perfect as Pak desired. But it began to glow with a deep amber light. It wove itself into the rhythm of Liara's song, turning its breaks into pauses, into a unique beat.

"It is not broken, Pak," Liara whispered. "It is singing its own song. The Garden needs this sound, or it will become the silence of a graveyard."

Pak stood in the shadows for a long time, watching her silhouette and the damaged Thread that now seemed the most vivid thing in the sector. He put his tools away in silence.

"Your methods have no scientific basis," he grumbled as he walked away.

But that night, he returned. He recalibrated the security systems to ensure no one would ever disturb that single reed.
